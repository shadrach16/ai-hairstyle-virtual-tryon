Best AI Hairstyle Apps in 2026: Complete Comparison

We downloaded and tested the top 10 hairstyle try-on apps to find which ones actually deliver realistic, usable results for all hair types.

What We Looked For

We evaluated each app on five criteria that matter most to real users:

  1. Realism: Does the hairstyle look natural on your face, or like a sticker?
  2. Inclusivity: Does it work for all hair textures, skin tones, and genders?
  3. Style variety: How many hairstyles are available?
  4. Speed: How quickly do you get results?
  5. Price: Is it actually usable without paying?

Our Top Picks for 2026

1. Hair Studio — Best Overall

Rating: 4.5/5

Hair Studio stands out for its combination of AI-powered realism and inclusive design. It's the only app in our testing that consistently produced natural-looking results across all hair textures — from straight European hair to 4C coily Afro-textured hair.

  • 100+ styles including braids, locs, fades, bobs, and pixie cuts
  • AI face mapping adapts styles to your unique features
  • Results in 3-5 seconds
  • Free tier with daily credits (no watermarks)
  • Weekly style updates following social media trends

Best for: Anyone who wants realistic results that work for their actual hair type.

2. FaceApp — Best for Quick Edits

Rating: 3.5/5

FaceApp is a general-purpose face editor with some hairstyle features. It's fast and well-known, but the hairstyle options are limited (~30 styles) and it struggles with textured or curly hair.

  • Quick processing time
  • Widely available (iOS + Android)
  • Limited free options, aggressive upselling

Best for: Quick experiments with straight hair styles.

3. YouCam Makeup — Best for Color

Rating: 3/5

YouCam excels at hair color changes but underwhelms with actual hairstyle changes. The "try-on" feature is more of a color overlay than a real style transformation.

  • Excellent hair color options
  • Good makeup features bundled in
  • Hairstyle options feel outdated
  • Results have watermarks on free tier

Best for: Trying new hair colors (not shapes/styles).

4. Hairstyle Try On — Basic Option

Rating: 2.5/5

A simple app that overlays hairstyle stickers on your photo. Results are clearly fake but it's completely free. Useful for a rough idea of shape, but don't expect realism.

5. Perfect365 — Fading Relevance

Rating: 2/5

Once popular, Perfect365 hasn't kept up with AI advances. The hairstyle feature feels like it's from 2019. Very few styles, no texture awareness.
